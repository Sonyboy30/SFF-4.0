Our work

We focus on the full picture of student success.

Learning matters, but so do confidence, well-being, stability, and access to new opportunities.

Focus area 01 | Education

Making learning more accessible

Students should have room to ask questions, explore interests, and build confidence.

We are interested in tutoring, mentorship, financial literacy, STEM, after-school learning, and other programs that make education feel useful and engaging.

TutoringMentorshipFinancial literacySTEM
Focus area 02 | Wellness

Helping students feel supported

Young people learn better when they feel safe, connected, and cared for.

We want to support programs that strengthen emotional well-being, belonging, healthy habits, and access to trusted adults.

BelongingMental wellnessHealthy habitsFamily support
Focus area 03 | Innovation

Testing ideas that could grow

Not every strong idea starts as a large program.

We are open to small pilots that solve a clear problem, track useful feedback, and have the potential to improve or expand over time.

Pilot programsCommunity researchProgram designLearning tools
What we look for

A strong idea usually has four things

A real need

The idea responds to something students, families, or educators are actually experiencing.

A clear plan

The organization knows what it wants to do and who will be responsible.

A useful goal

Success can be explained in a way that is realistic and easy to understand.

Room to learn

The team is willing to gather feedback and make the program better.
